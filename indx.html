Exercise 3: Labor Law Terminology Quiz

Choose the correct answer for each question:

1. What is a "collective bargaining agreement"?

A. A contract between an employer and an individual employee
B. A contract between an employer and a labor union representing employees
C. A legal document that outlines general labor laws in a country
D. A form that employees must sign when joining a company

2. What does "wrongful termination" refer to?

A. Firing an employee without giving proper notice
B. Firing an employee for a reason that violates employment law or contract terms
C. Terminating an entire department due to budget cuts
D. When an employee quits without giving proper notice

3. What is a "probationary period" in employment?

A. A time when an employee is being disciplined for poor performance
B. The time before an employee can join a labor union
C. An initial period of employment during which performance is evaluated
D. The time an employee must wait before receiving benefits

4. What is "severance pay"?

A. Compensation given to employees who are laid off or terminated without cause
B. Extra pay for working in dangerous conditions
C. Payment for unused vacation days
D. A penalty fee paid by employees who quit without notice

5. What is an "EEOC complaint"?

A. A grievance filed with a labor union
B. A complaint about workplace ethics
C. A formal charge of discrimination filed with the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission
D. A report about unsafe working conditions

Hint: A collective bargaining agreement involves multiple employees. Wrongful termination relates to illegal reasons for firing. Probationary periods happen when employees first join a company. Severance pay is provided when employment ends. The EEOC deals with discrimination issues.

Exercise 6: Labor Law Case Study Analysis

Read the following case study and answer the questions using advanced labor law terminology:

Case Study: Global Tech Solutions

Maria has been working at Global Tech Solutions for five years. Recently, the company introduced a new policy requiring employees to work on weekends without additional compensation. When Maria complained, her supervisor assigned her to less desirable projects and excluded her from important meetings. Last week, Maria discovered that male colleagues with less experience were receiving higher salaries. After filing a formal complaint with HR, Maria was told her position was being eliminated due to "restructuring." However, two weeks after her departure, the company hired a new person for a very similar role.

1. What type of discrimination might Maria be experiencing?

2. What is the legal term for the negative treatment Maria received after her complaint?

3. What type of termination might Maria claim she experienced?

4. What legal action could Maria potentially take against the company?

Hint: For question 1, think about the difference in pay between Maria and her male colleagues (gender discrimination). For question 2, consider what happens when an employee is treated poorly after making a complaint (retaliation). For question 3, think about terminations that happen for illegal reasons masked as legitimate ones (wrongful termination or constructive dismissal). For question 4, consider legal options like filing a complaint with government agencies or filing a lawsuit (EEOC complaint, wrongful termination lawsuit).
